iOS 6 and Earlier

When on the home screen of your iOS device, there is a screen to the left of this, which will read "Search iPhone" (or iPod, or iPad).


This is considered the "home screen" on your device. It usually contains default apps, such as Settings and App Store, on the first page unless you changed the layout of the screen.

Look at the area boxed in blue. The dots represent which screen page you are on. Since this is on the leftmost dot, this is on Page 1, which is the main home screen. You will notice the magnifying glass symbol to the left of this, which represents the Spotlight search page.

iOS 7

In iOS 7, there is no Spotlight search screen to the left of the home screen.
    

Instead, swipe down from anywhere on the home screen, and the search will appear at the top without taking you to a different screen. This will work on any home screen page. Do not swipe down too close to the top of the screen, or else Notification Center will be revealed instead.


Hopefully you are now able to use Spotlight, either on iOS or on the Mac.
